Of the possible "chicken-of-the-woods" species I found mine in the western US growing on a cut down eucalyptus tree it was most likely _Laetiporus gilbertsonii_ which has a reputation giving some people gastric upset and some more intense symptoms like dizziness as well. 

Secondly if you google "chicken-of-the-woods" and eucalyptus, you will see a few accounts of people saying that growing on eucalyptus itself this makes the mushroom even more toxic or more likely to cause issues. The theory being that eucalyptus trees have a lot of strong tanins/toxins and that they accumulate in the mushroom. 

However, given how mushroom digestion works and that any potential toxins would be pretty large molecules that would be subject to decomposing by the time they end up in the mushroom, I am much more sympathetic to the view that _Laetiporus gilbertsonii_ is just harder on people than the other, more edible, chicken-of-the-woods species. But who knows, I can't seem to find any real results on this. 

This page has a good note that sums up the splitting of the _Laetiporus_ genus pretty well. https://blog.mycology.cornell.edu/2006/10/31/eating-the-chicken-of-the-woods/

Also here is someone else's account who has cooked this mushroom many times and only once had a bad experience. This account is great because it shows that something is just up with these mushrooms and results tend to be mixed.
https://theartistsappetite.com/2023/09/15/chicken-of-the-woods/

So here is my account of how I prepared and ate it:
# How I prepared and cooked it
tldr: I removed the thickest portion in the middle as well as the kinda nasty and goopy far outer margin, I boiled it for 15 mins, and then breaded it and deep fried it. It was pretty good, but I probably under-salted for the boil leaving it a bit on the blander side. 

From the account linked above, I took note of the part about cutting away the tougher middle. This was a pretty young mushroom so there was not a ton to remove. But you can see below there is a raised portion and a outer edge portion, I wonder if I should have removed all the raised portion?

Knowing that this species might be hard on me I opted to boil the mushroom for 15 minutes ahead of time. I pretty much wanted to fully cook it 

As for the dredge and fry
Generally I followed this video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yLSPnyvuyuY

I boiled the mushroom for 15 minutes, flipping every 5. 


Cut off margin and base nodule
Boil in salted water for 15 mins flipping every 5 mins 

Batter with a dredge (chickpea flower simulates eggs) and a dry (panko) 
Do a double dredge!

# Results
My wife felt fine, I did in fact feel a bit queasy and while I was not dizzy, my vision was blurred or slow to refocus after movement . Full disclosure, I did have some wine, but not even close to enough to effect me in that way. Sorry this is not a clean experiment in that sense. I was just vibing man.
