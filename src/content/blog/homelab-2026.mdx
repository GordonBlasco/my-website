After a lot of learnings and a lot of pain with k8s, I am back to only running Proxmox on my homelab and paying for a cheap (16$ a month) VPS for my media server. This pretty much meets all my needs, saves me a lot of headaches, and for the short term, is cheaper than buying hardware in 2026.

Ultimately, I have hit the point where I don't want to manage a cluster anymore, specifically a k8s one. I loved learning and tinkering on my homelab, as a way to learn Kubernetes in a very hands-on way. And I am glad I spent that time, as I feel like it made me better at my job in almost every way and deepened my relationship with technology.

However, looking ahead, I could see that changing the system, keeping it secure, and staying on top of updates would probably add up to many hours of maintenance that would just not be a great use of my time, nor would I really enjoy it. As I move towards becoming a parent, I really cannot afford to spend my time fussing with anything homelab related. 

Second, two little mini pcs were never supposed to be the final cluster, it worked for learning, but it’s just not enough nodes to even reach a quorum, and I was already hitting memory limits with the small workloads I was running. Investing in scaling the system also felt increasingly silly with the increasing price of hardware.

So, all this has me rethinking what exactly I want out of a home server. And mainly I want a media server that always works, I want a local server to run some small services (e.g. bentoPDF), and I never want to think about port forwarding or any bullshit like that. 
Once prices come down, I will invest in a NAS system, but I will go with one that is more of a managed system like UGreen as a local backup for photos. 

I still have my dream of running my own data platform at home, but if I ever build that it will be fully separate from my home server and more of an experimental homelab.
